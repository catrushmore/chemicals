Much has been written on the technologies of household laundry, of doing for oneself or sending out, the mechanisation and drudgery, but stain removal seems to be not so well discussed amongst historians. I will start by examining stain removal techniques commonly suggested in British housekeeping manuals, newspaper and magazine columns and adverts for stain removing products, over the period 1930 to 1989. One chemical in particular, carbon tetrachloride is of special interest to me.  Carbon tetrachloride and brand name products based on this chemical became increasingly heavily promoted during World War Two, and remained a frequently seen advert into the 1950s. Used for both spot cleaning, or stain removal, and total dry cleaning of garment and accessories, the chemical kept cropping up in newspapers, and always with a tragic tale.
